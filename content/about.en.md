I'm a São Paulo native born in the 90s. I have the three most beautiful dogs in the world and the most incredible wife. I live in the toxic, chaotic, cosmopolitan and cyberpunk São Paulo, SP, Brazil.

Here on this blog I intend to write about all of my interests (there are many).

I've always been into **music**. I started playing acoustic guitar at 10, got my first electric guitar at 14. I went a long time without playing them because of college, but it was there that I discovered samba and the tamborim, which I played diligently for about 5 years, even performing at São Paulo's Carnival with a samba school and a street band. Around 30 I returned to string instruments. I dabble in bass and ukulele, have a small vinyl collection and love talking about music.

Because I'm a very disorganized yet methodical person, I research a lot about topics that fall under **"productivity"**, but my point is just to get organized without losing my mind, so I research things like, for example, taking better **notes** and **task management**.

As a teenager I've always been a technology enthusiast, having it as part of my hobbies. I'm currently tinkering with **self-hosting** and want to share about it.

I've always loved **games** of any kind since I was little. I learned a lot of English through **video games**, a hobby I picked up again in adulthood. I've also always enjoyed **board games**, but they're harder to play since they require people getting together. Recently I challenged myself to learn **Chess**, and I've been playing almost every day since then, even though I'm terrible at it.

I graduated with a Bachelor's degree in **Physics** from the University of São Paulo. Don't ask me why. But Physics taught me to see the world differently, to normalize the strange and unknown, to have technical rigor, and the importance of **Science** for society. I also ventured into subjects like **Philosophy of Science** and **Computer Music**. Outside the classroom, I actually learned even more. I learned about **class society**, about privilege, politics, feminism and activism.

Since being a scientist in Brazil is extremely challenging, after college I went to work with **Technology**, unsurprisingly. I started my career as an intern in **Software Engineering** and **Image Processing**, and later moved into the **Data** field, becoming a **Data Engineer**. I also did some work with **data analytics** and **data governance**, and ended up entering the world of **leadership and management**.

All these bolded terms may or may not be part of the content in this space. I don't commit to frequent posts, nor to specific topics. The world already demands enough productivity from us. This space is not about that.
